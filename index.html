About Me

My Path to Programming

I have taken quite a journey to discover my passion for programming. Always having an affinity and an aptitude for "computery" things—building PCs in my youth, becoming a power-user of every platform and application I came across—I had simply considered this interest to be a hobby. In fact, my higher education and work experience are in mostly unrelated fields (interestingly, unrelated to each other, as well).

Pursuing, and acquiring, a Master's degree in Music took me to New York City, where I lived and worked for almost 20 years. As a young musician, I supplemented my income through work in the financial services industry. Over time, I eventually found the business world to be vastly more lucrative, waking up one day to realize my job had become a full-fledged career. For 15 years, I worked in desktop publishing and graphic design departments at preeminent financial firms in NYC, starting as an entry-level word processor, adding graphic design to my skills-set, becoming a trainer, and then organically transitioning into management as an Operations Manager, then a Senior Account Manager being groomed for Director.

A handful of years ago, my wife had an opportunity to relocate to Portland, OR, through her employer. This was an interesting moment to reassess our lives (some might call it the proverbial mid-life crisis!) and, even though it meant me leaving a long and successful career, we decided to make the move—packing up everything and moving 3,000 miles across the country. The move afforded me the opportunity to evaluate where to take the next half of my life. It also gave me a lot of time to pursue personal interests, the majority of which naturally revolve around technology. Given the deep, deep ocean that is technology, I found myself studying and hopping from one topic to the next—command-line computing, networking, UNIX, Linux (already being well acquainted with Windows and macOS), Ethical Hacking, and eventually programming.

One of many ethical hacking courses I took introduced me to the Python programming language, using it to write our own custom tools. This was my first experience with programming and I was so enthralled with it that I spent the entire next year becoming fluent in Python and devouring any material I could on associated programming concepts. Surprisingly, it wasn't until late in this process of self-education that I realized this, THIS, is how I want to spend the rest of my professional life! So, I am presently immersed in a 6-month bootcamp at Epicodus code school and I'm elated to be stepping into the wonderful world of software development.
