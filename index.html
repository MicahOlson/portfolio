About Me

I have taken quite a journey to discover my passion for programming. Always having an affinity and an aptitude for "computery" things—building PCs in my youth, becoming a power-user of every platform and application I came across—I had simply considered this a hobby. In fact, my higher education and work experience are in a mostly unrelated fields (interestingly, unrelated from each other, as well).

Persuing, and acquiring, a Master's degree in Music took me to New York City, where I lived and worked for almost 20 years. As a young musician, I supplimented my income through work in the financial services industry. Over time, I eventually found the business world to be vastly more lucrative, waking up one day to realize it had become a full-fledged career. For 15 years, I worked in desktop publishing and graphic design departments at preiminent financial firms in NYC, starting as an entry-level word processor, adding graphic design to my skills-set, becoming a trainer, and then ulimately into a management track as an Operations Manager, then a Senior Account Manager quickly on my way to Director.

Then one day, my wife had an opportunity to relocate to Portland, OR, through her employer. This was an interesting moment to reassess our lives (some might call it the proverbial mid-life crisis!) and, even though it meant leaving a long and successful career, we decided to make the move—packing up everything and moving 3,000 miles across the country.
